WE BELONG TOGETHER:

Analyzing the Harry Styles fandom and Social Identity Theory

Social identity theory is the idea that we each fall into a multitude of groups that form our unique identity and that these groups change how we view ourselves and others. Through categorization, identification, and comparison we are able to define who we are and which places we feel we belong. This applies heavily to entertainment media when we view how media figures influence how we behave, interact with others, and think of others. The core of social identity theory relies on there being an in-group and an out-group, which can become problematic in some areas of fandoms. Fandom culture and parasocial relationship theory are based on the identification with certain figures, and how this contributes to our self-concept. Both fandoms and parasocial relationships can increase a sense of belonging as well as promote communities amongst their fans. For our evaluation of social identity theory with its ties to fandoms and parasocial relationships, we wanted to dive into the fandom of Harry Styles and how it shapes who they are, especially after the release of his latest album.
